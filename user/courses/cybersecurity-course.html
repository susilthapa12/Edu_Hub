Introduction to Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

Why Cybersecurity is Important

  • Protect sensitive data from unauthorized access
  • Ensure privacy and security for users
  • Prevent financial loss from cyber attacks
  • Maintain trust and reputation of organizations
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements

Common Cyber Threats

  • Malware (viruses, worms, trojans)
  • Phishing attacks
  • Ransomware attacks
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS) attacks
  • SQL Injection
  • Zero-day exploits
  • Man-in-the-Middle attacks

Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Use strong, unique passwords and a password manager
  • Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)
  • Keep software and systems up-to-date
  • Regularly back up important data
  • Be cautious of suspicious emails and links
  • Use antivirus and firewall protections
  • Educate yourself and employees about cybersecurity threats
